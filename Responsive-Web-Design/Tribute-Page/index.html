Sir Charles Kao Kuen

Important Dates

  • 1933: Born on November 4, in Shanghai, China.
  • 1948: Moved to Hong Kong due to the Chinese Civil War.
  • 1952: Graduated from St. Joseph's College in Hong Kong.
  • 1957: Earned a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Woolwich Polytechnic (now the University of Greenwich) in London.
  • 1965: Joined the Standard Telecommunication Laboratories (STL) in Harlow, England.
  • 1966: Published groundbreaking research on the potential of fiber optic communication in the paper "Dielectric-fibre surface waveguides for optical frequencies," co-authored with George Hockham.
  • 1966: Demonstrated that impurities in glass were the primary cause of signal loss in optical fibers, leading to the realization that purified glass fibers could be used for long-distance communication.
  • 1967: Proposed the use of highly purified glass for fiber optics, significantly advancing the technology.
  • 1970: Successfully demonstrated the transmission of light signals through fiber optic cables over long distances.
  • 1974: Appointed as the Director of Engineering at ITT Corporation's electro-optical products division in Roanoke, Virginia, USA.
  • 1982: Became the Vice-Chancellor of the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK).
  • 1987: Co-founded the Technology Centre for Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK).
  • 1996: Retired from CUHK but continued to serve as an honorary professor and a senior research fellow.
  • 2009: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for "groundbreaking achievements concerning the transmission of light in fibers for optical communication."
  • 2010: Diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease, leading to increased public awareness of the condition.
  • 2018: Passed away on September 23, in Hong Kong.
