Sir Charles Kao Kuen

Important Dates

1933Born on November 4, in Shanghai, China. 1948Moved to Hong Kong due to the Chinese Civil War. 1952Graduated from St. Joseph's College in Hong Kong. 1957Earned a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from Woolwich Polytechnic (now the University of Greenwich) in London. 1965Joined the Standard Telecommunication Laboratories (STL) in Harlow, England. 1966Published groundbreaking research on the potential of fiber optic communication in the paper "Dielectric-fibre surface waveguides for optical frequencies," co-authored with George Hockham. 1966Demonstrated that impurities in glass were the primary cause of signal loss in optical fibers, leading to the realization that purified glass fibers could be used for long-distance communication. 1967Proposed the use of highly purified glass for fiber optics, significantly advancing the technology. 1970Successfully demonstrated the transmission of light signals through fiber optic cables over long distances. 1974Appointed as the Director of Engineering at ITT Corporation's electro-optical products division in Roanoke, Virginia, USA. 1982Became the Vice-Chancellor of the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK). 1987Co-founded the Technology Centre for Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK). 1996Retired from CUHK but continued to serve as an honorary professor and a senior research fellow. 2009Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for "groundbreaking achievements concerning the transmission of light in fibers for optical communication." 2010Diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease, leading to increased public awareness of the condition. 2018Passed away on September 23, in Hong Kong.
